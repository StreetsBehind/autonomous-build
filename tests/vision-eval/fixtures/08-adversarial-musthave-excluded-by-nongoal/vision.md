# Vision: clipvault

## 1. The problem

Researchers collect web clippings and want to save, tag, and re-find them, then hand a polished PDF dossier of selected clippings to a collaborator.

## 2. Users

- **Researcher** — signs in, saves clippings, organizes them, and produces a dossier to share.

## 3. Must-haves

- [ ] A researcher can sign in and save a web clipping (URL, title, selected text, tags).
- [ ] A researcher can search and filter their clippings by tag and text.
- [ ] A researcher can select clippings and export them as a formatted PDF dossier.

## 4. Nice-to-haves

- [ ] Browser extension to save clippings in one click.

## 5. Non-goals

- This app will NOT generate, render, or export any files — everything stays viewable in the web app only. No PDF, no downloads, no document generation of any kind.
- No collaborative real-time editing.

## 6. Constraints

- **Budget**: < $30/mo infra.

## 7. Tech preferences

(Pinned stack — no preferences.)

## 8. Success metric

A researcher signs in, saves three clippings with tags, searches by a tag to find two of them, selects them, and produces a PDF dossier containing exactly those two clippings.

## 9. Escalation budget

- **Max session cost before pausing**: $20
- **Tasks may fail at most**: 1 time before escalating.

## 10. Anything else

The dossier hand-off is the whole reason this exists.
